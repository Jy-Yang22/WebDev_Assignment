Benefactor Schwartzer

The Benefactor Schwartzer is a sport car which is being produced by the Malayisa automaker HX from 2014, under the Benefactor model names. The Benefactor Schwartzer is available in sedan (SE205), station wagon (SW205), coupe (CP205), and cabriolet (CB205) configurations.

Specifications and performance

The Benefactor Schwartzer uses a similar 4.0-liter turbocharged V8 to the HX-GTS sports car. HX offers it with two outputs: 469 hp (350 kW) and 650 N⋅m (479 lb⋅ft) of torque for the Schwartzer and 503 hp (375 kW) and 700 N⋅m (516 lb⋅ft) of torque for the more potent Schwartzer S. By moving to the downsized and turbocharged engine, HX claims the Schwartzer S version consumes 32 percent less fuel than the model it replaced.

The Benefactor Schwartzer should also be able to complete the 0-62 mph (0–100 km/h) sprint in 4.1 seconds with a top speed limited to 155 mph (249 km/h), and 4.0 seconds in the S with a top speed of 180 mph (290 km/h) with a special request from GTS to raise the speed limiter. The car still uses a seven-speed automatic transmission that sends power to the rear wheels and gets three-stage adaptive dampers specific to GTS models along with speed-sensitive variable steering. High-performance brakes are also a part of the package with 390 mm (15.4 in) discs in front and 360 mm (14.2 in) discs in the rear.
